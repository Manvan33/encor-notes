6.4 - DNA Center

4 DNA Center components:

  • Design with physical maps and logical topologies.
  • Policy to define user and device profiles that facilitate highly secure access and network segmentation.
  • Provisioning for policy-based automation to deliver services to the network.
  • Assurance to combine deep insights with rich context to deliver a consistent experience and proactively optimize your network.

These are new Cisco DNA Center capabilities:

  • AIOps is an AI-driven visibility, observability, insights, and troubleshooting to ensure the health of your users, applications, and infrastructure.
  • NetOps is an automation to simplify the creation and maintenance of your networks with flexibility to move from manual to AI-assisted to selectively autonomous network management.
  • SecOps is an AI-driven security to classify endpoints and enforce security policies for a complete zero trust workplace solution.
  • DevOps is a mature APIs, software development kits (SDKs), and closed-loop integrations to simplify and streamline ecosystem integration.

The following is the suggested workflow for Cisco DNA Center design:

  1. Create a hierarchy that consists of areas, buildings, and floors.
  2. Define global network settings, for example, authentication, authorization, and accounting (AAA), DHCP, DNS, and Network Time Protocol (NTP).
  3. Define the “golden image” to ensure consistency in your network.
  4. Create templates to automate applying commonly used configuration to the devices.
  5. Define network profiles to apply the templates to the devices.
  6. Assign devices to specific locations, for example, a building.

Extensibility
...

The Cisco DNA Center open platform for Intent-Based Networking (IBN) offers 360-degree extensibility through multiple components.

  • Intent-based APIs enable communication between the controller, business, and IT applications, allowing the network to align with business needs.
  • Process adapters streamline integration with IT and network systems, supporting services like ITSM, IPAM, and reporting systems.
  • Domain adapters ensure a consistent intent-based infrastructure across domains such as data center, WAN, and security.
  • SDKs extend management to third-party vendor devices, supporting diverse environments and allowing recognition and management of unknown devices.

Inventory
...

For discovery, DNAC can use LLDP, CDP or an IP range (/20 maximum). It selects one IP address as the management IP, connects to the device and uses LLDP again.
You need to provide a list of device credentials to be used by DNAC.

When a device is discovered, Cisco DNA Center configures the following functions:

  • SNMP credentials and SNMP trap receiver
  • NETCONF credentials
  • IP device tracking
  • Certificate trustpoint
  • Cisco TrustSec credentials
  • Wireless network assurance
